How Does a Telecommunications Review Work?

Understanding the process, what we analyse, and what outcomes you can expect from an independent telco review.

Summary: A telecommunications review typically starts with understanding your current providers, services, usage and goals. We then analyse your bills and contracts, compare the market for alternatives, and present options in a clear, impartial way. You receive recommendations based on fit and value—not on which provider we favour. This article explains the steps and what to expect for Australian businesses in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart and beyond.

What Is a Telecommunications Review?

A telecommunications review is an independent assessment of your business’s phone, internet, mobile and related services. The aim is to give you visibility over what you are spending, how your current setup compares to the market, and what options exist if you are unhappy with your incumbent or simply want to check you are on the right plans. We do not sell carrier services; we compare providers so you can make an informed decision.

Step 1: Understanding Your Situation

We start by gathering information about your current setup. That usually includes: which providers you are with, what services you use (e.g. business phone, NBN, mobile, 1300/1800 numbers, contact centre), how your staff and offices communicate, and any issues you have with support or service levels. We also look at your current bills and contracts so we understand spend, contract end dates and any constraints (e.g. early exit fees). This discovery phase ensures our comparison is relevant to your size, locations and priorities.

Step 2: Bill and Contract Analysis

We review your bills to see what you are paying for each service, whether there are errors or overcharges, and whether you are on plans that still match your usage. Contract terms—length, renewal dates, price rises and exit conditions—are also important. This analysis often reveals opportunities to renegotiate, downgrade or upgrade, or to plan for a switch at contract end. For businesses with multiple sites across Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart or regional areas, we can break down spend by site or service so you see the full picture.

Step 3: Market Comparison

Using our knowledge of the Australian market, we compare what other providers typically offer for your type of requirements. We look at typical pricing, features, service levels and contract terms. We do not favour one vendor; we present a range of options so you can see the trade-offs. If your current provider is not meeting your needs, we help you understand what alternatives can offer—without making negative claims about your incumbent.

Step 4: Recommendations and Next Steps

We collate the findings into a clear assessment and, where appropriate, recommendations. These might be: stay and renegotiate, switch to a different provider, consolidate services onto one bill, or change technology (e.g. move to a hosted phone system or different NBN tier). You then decide; we can support with procurement or transition planning if needed. Our goal is to put forward options that work and represent value for money.

Who Benefits From a Review?

Any Australian business that wants clarity on its telecommunications spend and options can benefit—whether you are in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart, Perth or elsewhere. Reviews are especially useful when contracts are due for renewal, when you have outgrown your current setup, or when you are experiencing poor service or support and are considering a change.

FAQ

How long does a review take?

Typical timelines depend on the scope (e.g. one service vs full estate) and how quickly we receive bills and contract details. Many reviews are completed within a few weeks once information is to hand.

Do you charge for the review?

Engagements are scoped and quoted separately. Contact us to discuss your situation and how we would approach a review.

Can we keep our existing numbers?

Number portability in Australia generally allows you to keep phone and mobile numbers when switching providers. We can factor this into any recommendation.
