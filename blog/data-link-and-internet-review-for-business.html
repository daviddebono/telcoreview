Data Link and Internet Review for Business

Reviewing connectivity to match your organisation’s current and future requirements.

Summary: A data link and internet review examines how well your current connectivity supports your business. It looks at capacity, resilience, performance and cost, and considers how requirements may change over the life of your contracts.

Why review connectivity?

Connectivity underpins cloud services, collaboration, voice and customer-facing systems. As usage patterns evolve—more remote work, more cloud applications—it is important to check whether your existing data links and internet services still fit.

A review can highlight whether capacity is adequate, where resilience might need to be improved and whether the commercial structure reflects how you actually use the services.

Key elements of a data link and internet review

1. Inventory and topology

Begin by documenting your connectivity footprint: access types, speeds, locations and how links are interconnected. For organisations with multiple sites, this includes any hub-and-spoke or mesh arrangements, as well as links into cloud environments or data centres.

2. Capacity and utilisation

Review utilisation data for key links where it is available, or use application and user growth as a proxy when detailed statistics are not accessible. The aim is to understand where links are comfortably sized, where there may be headroom and where congestion is likely.

3. Resilience and diversity

Assess how connectivity would behave under failure scenarios. Do critical sites have diverse paths or backup options? Are failover arrangements tested? A review documents current resilience and identifies options proportionate to the business impact of downtime.

4. Commercial structure

Connect costs to capacity and risk. Consider whether current pricing and terms are in line with market norms for similar services. This can inform negotiations at renewal or help frame a broader telecommunications review.

Using review outcomes

Outcomes from a data link and internet review can feed into network redesign, cloud migration planning or broader telecommunications strategy. They provide a clearer basis for decisions about where to invest in increased capacity or improved resilience, and where savings may be possible.
